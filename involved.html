Frequently Asked Questions

What types of partnerships does Ni-Wakati Sports Africa offer?

+

We offer various partnership models including: Corporate Sponsorships (financial and in-kind), Program Partnerships (co-designing and implementing sports initiatives), NGO Collaborations (joint projects and resource sharing), Government Partnerships (policy development and national programs), and Academic Partnerships (research and student placements). Each partnership is tailored to align with your organization's goals while maximizing social impact.

Can I volunteer remotely or do I need to be in Africa?

+

We offer both on-ground and virtual volunteering opportunities. Virtual volunteers can contribute through skills like digital marketing, graphic design, content writing, data analysis, research, grant writing, and social media management. On-ground volunteers work directly in communities across Africa. We match volunteers based on skills, interests, and location preferences.

How are donations used and what's the impact?

+

85% of donations go directly to program implementation, 10% to organizational capacity building, and 5% to administrative costs. Your donation supports: Youth sports programs ($25 provides equipment for 5 children), Coach training ($50 trains a community coach), Sports tournaments ($100 supports a youth tournament), Facility improvements ($250 upgrades community sports spaces), and Program expansion ($500 launches new initiatives). All donors receive annual impact reports.

What skills are needed for volunteering?

+

We need diverse skills including: Sports coaching and training, Event management, Community mobilization, Digital skills (social media, design, video editing), Research and data analysis, Grant writing and fundraising, Translation (local languages), Professional services (legal, accounting, marketing), and Educational support (tutoring, mentoring). We provide training for all volunteers regardless of experience level.

How are partnerships structured and measured?

+

Partnerships begin with a discovery phase, followed by co-creation of objectives and a formal agreement. We establish clear KPIs including: Number of youth reached, Community engagement metrics, Skill development outcomes, Health and wellness improvements, Economic impact (jobs created, local spending), and Sustainability measures. Partners receive quarterly reports and participate in annual partnership reviews. Minimum commitment periods vary by partnership type.
